The Vedic Path: From Ancient Theory to the Sahasranāmam

The Three Guṇas

The discourse begins with the three guṇas: Sattvaguṇa (Calm), Rajoguṇa (Restless), and Tamoguṇa (Lethargic). The Vedas provide means to fulfill desires arising from these qualities.

Role of the Guru

The Vedas are indirect. A Guru is essential to interpret mantras like the Śānti Mantra, explaining how Bhagavān remains complete throughout the 14 lokas.

Itihāsas as Practical Proof

The ṛṣis composed Purāṇas and Itihāsas as practical demonstrations of Vedic theory. For example, Rāmacandra demonstrated worshipping one's father as a god.

The Supremacy of Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam and the Chanting Path

Reasons for Greatness

Ādi Śaṅkarācārya cites: Mahābhārata-sāratvāt (Essence of the 5th Veda), Bhīṣma-parigrahāt (Final opinion of a supreme devotee), and more.

The Ṣaḍripu

We must conquer Kāma, Krodha, Lobha, Moha, Mada, and Mātsarya. These internal enemies are more dangerous than external ones.

Predestination vs. Free Will

While events are based on Prārabdha Karma, how we react is our choice. Humans are given intellect to choose the right path.

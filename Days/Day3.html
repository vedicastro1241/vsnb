Day 3: Echoes of Kurukṣetra

The Birthplace: Kurukṣetra

The journey brings us to Kurukṣetra, the exact location where Bhīṣma lay on the śarapañjara (bed of arrows). Here, the genesis of the Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam took place in the 149th chapter of the Mahābhārata.

Prelude & Gratitude

The prelude consists of six ślokas:

  • Removal of Obstacles: Meditating on Viṣṇu as śuklāmbaradharaṃ (wearing white) with a prasannavadanaṃ (benign smile).
  • Lineage of Vyāsa: Vasiṣṭha → Śakti → Parāśara → Vyāsa → Śuka. Vyāsa is revered as a manifestation of Viṣṇu (vyāsāya viṣṇu rūpāya).
The Protector of the Womb: Kṛṣṇa saved the lineage by protecting Parīkṣit (Viṣṇurāta) in Uttarā's womb from Aśvatthāmā's Brahmāstra.

The Arrival of Kaliyuga

As Kṛṣṇa left the earth, Kaliyuga began. King Parīkṣit encountered Kali tormenting a bull (Dharma) standing on one leg (representing Truth/Charity in this age). Kali was granted five places: Gambling, Alcohol, Licentiousness, Butchery, and Gold.

The Curse & The Bhāgavatam

Influenced by gold, Parīkṣit insulted Sage Śamīka and was cursed by the son, Śṛṅgī, to die by the snake Takṣaka in seven days. This led the King to the banks of the Gaṅgā, where Śuka Parabrahmā narrated the Śrīmad Bhāgavatam.

Legacy: To hear his ancestors' history, Parīkṣit's son, Janamejaya, prompted the narration of the entire Mahābhārata by Sage Vaiśampāyana.

The Heart of the Sahasranāmam

Within the Śānti Parva, Yudhiṣṭhira, filled with grief, asks Bhīṣma six profound questions:

  1. Kim ekaṃ daivataṃ loke: Who is the one supreme deity?
  2. Kiṃ vāpyekaṃ parāyaṇaṃ: Who is the ultimate refuge?
  3. Stuvantaḥ kaṃ: Who should be praised?
  4. Kam arcantaḥ: Who should be worshipped?
  5. Ko dharmaḥ paramo mataḥ: What is the supreme dharma?
  6. Kiṃ japan mucyate: Chanting whose name brings liberation?

Dharma & The Influence of Food

Bhīṣma explains his previous silence during Draupadī's humiliation: he was fed by Duryodhana, and adharmic food clouded his mind. Now, lying on arrows, that "bad blood" had drained, leaving him pure to teach.

Attributes of the Lord

  • Anidhanam: Without end.
  • Anantam: Limitless in Time, Space, and Objects.
  • Lokādhyakṣaṃ: The supreme witness of the 14 worlds.
Simple Worship: Bhagavān accepts a leaf, flower, fruit, or water (patraṃ, puṣpaṃ, phalaṃ, toyaṃ) if offered with pure love, as demonstrated by Kṛṣṇa eating banana skins at Vidura's home.

Viṣṇu: The Source of Purity

Bhīṣma concludes by identifying Mahāviṣṇu as the source of all auspiciousness (maṅgalaṃ). He is the creator of the Vedas (brahman) and the manifestation of Dharma itself.

The Lotus-Eyed Lord

He is called Puṇḍarīkākṣa. His eyes are red like lotuses because:

  • They reflect his joy for devotees or anger toward enemies.
  • They reflect the image of Mahālakṣmī, who resides in a forest of lotuses.
The Final Answer: The supreme deity, the ultimate refuge, and the father of all is Mahāviṣṇu. Worshiping Him is the "best dharma" and the path to crossing the cycle of birth and death.

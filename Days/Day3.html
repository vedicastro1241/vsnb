Shri. Ramabhadran V Guruji

Day 3: Echoes of Kurukṣetra

Journey to the Birthplace

The discourse begins with a spiritual journey to Kurukṣetra, the exact location where Bhīṣma lay on the śarapañjara (bed of arrows). The speaker notes the lineage of Vyāsa: He is the great-grandson of Vasiṣṭha, grandson of Śakti, son of Parāśara, and father of Śuka.

vyāsāya viṣṇu rūpāya: Vyāsa is identified as a manifestation of Mahāviṣṇu, who is victorious over both external enemies like Kaṃsa and internal enemies like the cycle of saṃsāra.

Prelude: Removing Obstacles

The first two ślokas of the Sahasranāmam are for vighna-nivāraṇa. We meditate on Viṣṇu: śuklāmbaradharaṃ (wearing white), śaśivarṇaṃ (moon-colored), and prasannavadanaṃ (clear, smiling face).

The Birth of Parīkṣit

Aśvatthāmā directed a Brahmāstra toward Uttarā's womb. Uttarā prayed to Kṛṣṇa (pāhi pāhi mahāyogin), who entered the womb to neutralize the weapon. Because the child "examined" (parīkṣā) everyone to find that same divine form he saw in the womb, he was named **Parīkṣit**.

The Five Places of Kali

As Kaliyuga began, King Parīkṣit encountered Kali tormenting a bull (Dharma) standing on one leg (Truth/Charity). Kali requested places to stay, and the King granted five:

  • Dyūtaṃ: Gambling.
  • Pānaṃ: Alcohol consumption.
  • Striyaḥ: Licentiousness.
  • Sūnā: Animal butchery.
  • Suvarṇa: Gold/Excessive attachment to wealth.

Because the King wore a golden crown, Kali influenced his mind, leading him to place a dead snake on Śamīka Maharṣi, which resulted in the curse of death within seven days.

Birth of the Śrīmad Bhāgavatam

To seek liberation in his final seven days, Parīkṣit met Śuka Parabrahmā. Their dialogue became the 12 skandhas of the Bhāgavatam. Later, his son Janamejaya performed a sarpa-satra (snake sacrifice), but was stopped by Vaiśampāyana, who then narrated the Mahābhārata.

The Collaboration: Vyāsa & Gaṇeśa

Vyāsa sought Gaṇeśa as his scribe. Gaṇeśa agreed on the condition that Vyāsa never stop dictating. Vyāsa added a counter-condition: Gaṇeśa must understand every verse before writing.

  • The Strategy: Vyāsa dictated complex philosophical ślokas to gain "free time" while Gaṇeśa reflected.
  • The Sarasvatī: The river was so noisy that Vyāsa requested her to go underground (antah-salilā) so they could work in silence at the Vyāsa Guhā.

Yudhiṣṭhira’s Quest for Truth

Following the war, Yudhiṣṭhira was filled with grief. Kṛṣṇa led him to Bhīṣma, describing him as the "epitome of dharma." Yudhiṣṭhira asked six specific questions:

  1. kimekaṃ daivataṃ loke: Who is the one supreme deity?
  2. kiṃ vāpyekaṃ parāyaṇaṃ: Who is the one ultimate refuge?
  3. stuvantaḥ kaṃ: Who should be praised through hymns?
  4. kamarcantaḥ: Who should be worshipped through rituals?
  5. ko dharmaḥ sarvadharmāṇāṃ: Which is the supreme dharma among all?
  6. kiṃ japan mucyate jantuḥ: Chanting whose name liberates a being from the saṃsāra cycle?

The Influence of Food on Thought

Draupadī questioned why Bhīṣma remained silent during her humiliation. Bhīṣma explained that he was then being fed by Duryodhana. Because he consumed adharmic food, his mind was clouded. As he lay on the bed of arrows, that "bad blood" had drained, allowing him to teach in a pure state.

Answering the Limitless Lord

Bhīṣma identifies the Lord as Jagatprabhu and Anantam (Limitless):

  • Time: He is anādi (no beginning) and anidhanam (no end).
  • Space: He is all-pervading (viṣṇu), like fire that exists everywhere simultaneously.
  • Objects: He resides in all things—gold, silver, or wood.
Bhagavān accepts even a leaf (patraṃ) or a drop of water (toyaṃ) if offered with love. Recall Vidura, who offered banana skins in his pure excitement, which Kṛṣṇa happily accepted.

The Supreme Dharma & Refuge

Bhīṣma concludes that the highest dharma is to praise and worship the Lord with affection. He is Puṇḍarīkākṣa (Lotus-eyed) because He looks at His devotees with happiness, and because the image of Mahālakṣmī is reflected in His eyes.

The Ultimate Source

  • Supreme Refuge: He is the abode all must enter, performing eternal penance as Nara-Nārāyaṇa in Badrināth.
  • Supreme Deity: He is the source of all purity. When His toe reached Brahmaloka, the water from His abhiṣeka became the holy Gaṅgā.
  • The Witness: He is the lokādhyakṣaṃ (supreme witness) of the 14 worlds.

By constantly chanting the thousand names, a jīvātmā crosses over all sorrows (duḥkhātigo bhavet) and is liberated from the bonds of birth and death.

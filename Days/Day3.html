Day 3: Echoes of Kurukṣetra: The Genesis

The Prelude: Removing Obstacles

The discourse begins at Kurukṣetra, where Bhīṣma lay on the śarapañjara (bed of arrows). Before the stotra, we meditate on Mahāviṣṇu as śuklāmbaradharaṃ (wearing white), śaśivarṇaṃ (moon-colored), with four hands and a prasannavadanaṃ (clear, smiling face).

The Lineage of Vyāsa

Gratitude is offered to Vyāsa Bhagavān, identifying him as vyāsāya viṣṇu rūpāya (a manifestation of Viṣṇu). His lineage is detailed as:

  • Great-grandson of Vasiṣṭha
  • Grandson of Śakti
  • Son of Parāśara
  • Father of Śuka (notably mentioned as "father of brahma")
Context: The Sahasranāmam is found in the 149th chapter of the Mahābhārata, revealed after the Pāṇḍavas' victory.

Birth of Parīkṣit and the Arrival of Kali

After Aśvatthāmā directed a Brahmāstra toward Uttarā's womb, Kṛṣṇa entered to neutralize it. The child, having "examined" (parīkṣā) everyone to find that divine form, was named Parīkṣit.

The Five Abodes of Kali

As Kaliyuga began, Parīkṣit encountered Kali tormenting a bull (Dharma) standing on one leg (Satya/Dāna). Parīkṣit granted Kali five places:

  1. Dyūtaṃ: Gambling
  2. Pānaṃ: Alcohol
  3. Striyaḥ: Licentiousness
  4. Sūnā: Animal butchery
  5. Suvarṇa: Excessive attachment to gold

Due to the gold in his crown, Kali influenced the King to place a dead snake on Śamīka Maharṣi, leading to a curse by Śṛṅgī to die in 7 days via the snake Takṣaka.

The Scribe and the Underground River

Vyāsa sought Gaṇeśa as a scribe for the Mahābhārata. A mutual condition was set: Vyāsa must not stop dictating, and Gaṇeśa must understand every verse before writing.

The Strategy: Vyāsa dictated complex philosophical ślokas to gain "free time" to compose new ones while Gaṇeśa reflected. This occurred in the Vyāsa Guhā near Badrināth. The Sarasvatī river was requested to go underground (antah-salilā) as her noise disrupted the work.

In the aftermath of the curse, Śukadeva narrated the 12 skandhas of the Śrīmad Bhāgavatam to Parīkṣit, leading to his liberation. Later, Janamejaya’s snake sacrifice was stopped by Vaiśampāyana, who then narrated the Mahābhārata.

Yudhiṣṭhira’s Six Sacred Questions

Filled with grief in the Śānti Parva, Yudhiṣṭhira approaches Bhīṣma, the "epitome of dharma," with six inquiries:

Question #Sanskrit InquiryMeaning
1kimekaṃ daivataṃ lokeWho is the one supreme deity?
2kiṃ vāpyekaṃ parāyaṇaṃWho is the ultimate refuge/destination?
3stuvantaḥ kaṃWho should be praised through hymns?
4kamarcantaḥWho should be worshipped through rituals?
5ko dharmaḥ paramo mataḥWhich is the supreme dharma among all?
6kiṃ japan mucyateChanting whose name brings liberation?

The Influence of Food & Dharma

Draupadī questioned Bhīṣma's silence during her humiliation. Bhīṣma explained that because he consumed food from an adharmic source (Duryodhana), his mind was clouded. However, as "bad blood" drained on the bed of arrows, he returned to a dharmic position to teach.

Attributes of the Supreme

  • Anantam (Limitless): Not limited by Time (anādi/anidhanam), Space (all-pervading fire analogy), or Objects (resides in all).
  • Jagatprabhu: Lord of the universe that is born, exists, and dissolves.
Simple Worship: Referencing the Gītā, He accepts a leaf (patraṃ), flower (puṣpaṃ), fruit (phalaṃ), or water (toyaṃ).

The Supreme Refuge and Purity

Bhīṣma establishes Mahāviṣṇu as the mahad bhūtam (great element) from which all jīvātmās originate. He is the greatest performer of Tapas (as Nara-Nārāyaṇa in the Himalayas).

Why Lotus Eyes? (Puṇḍarīkākṣa)

Two reasons: 1. Happiness when looking at devotees/anger at enemies. 2. Constant reflection of the reddish Mahālakṣmī (a forest of lotuses) in His eyes.

The Source of Purity

During the Vāmana-Trivikrama avatar, the water from Brahmā's abhiṣeka on the Lord’s toe became the Gaṅgā. He is the source of all maṅgalaṃ (auspiciousness) and the father of all beings.

Result: By chanting the 1000 names, a soul crosses all sorrows (duḥkhātigo bhavet) and is liberated from saṃsāra.

Day 5: The Cosmic Form and Grace of Śrī Kṛṣṇa

Technical Framework & Purpose of Pralaya

The discourse recaps the pūrvāṅgam. Bhīṣma emphasizes that these names were seen by sages (ṛṣis) and compiled as a garland. Every mantra requires:

  • Ṛṣi: Vyāsa Bhagavān (the seer).
  • Chandas: Anuṣṭup (4 padas, 8 syllables each).
  • Devatā: Śrī Kṛṣṇa (Devakīnandanaḥ).
  • Bījam & Śakti: The Lord's immortality and the strength Kṛṣṇa gave to Bhīṣma.
Why Pralaya? Just as the body needs night for rest, the jīvātmā needs a period of rest in pralaya before the next cycle toward liberation. Chanting is done Mahāviṣṇu-prītyarthe—for His pleasure.

The Cosmic Form (Viśvarūpa)

Mahāviṣṇu's body encompasses the entire universe. All beings—humans, birds (khaga), cattle (go), and snakes (bhogi)—reside within Him.

Historical Mantras

The discourse highlights the ṣaḍakṣari (6 syllables), aṣṭākṣari (8 syllables), and dvādaśākṣarī (12 syllables). 5-year-old Dhruva chanted the 12-syllable mantra in Madhuvan and received darśana in just 6 months.

Deep Analysis: "Śāntākāraṃ"

This śloka describes the peaceful form of the Lord on the Milky Ocean (kṣīrasāgara) with four arms (caturbhuja):

  • Śāntākāraṃ: Form of absolute peace/bliss.
  • Bhujagaśayanaṃ: Resting on the serpent Ādiśeṣa.
  • Viśvadhāraṃ: The support of the universe (like gold to jewelry).
  • Gaganasadṛśaṃ / Meghavarṇaṃ: Vast like the blue sky; dark like a rain cloud full of compassion (karuṇā).
  • Kamalanayana: Lotus eyes. Their reddish hue comes from looking at Mahālakṣmī (whose reddish form is reflected) or when fighting in anger.
Bhavabhayaharam: The Lord removes the fear of the unknown future (anxieties about health/jobs).

The Test of Bhṛgu & the Śrīvatsa

The Śrīvatsa is a mark on the right side of the Lord's chest, signifying the residence of Mahālakṣmī. Its origin lies in Bhṛgu Maharṣi's test of the Trinity:

Sages sent Bhṛgu to find the most sāttvika deity. When he reached Vaikuṇṭha and found Viṣṇu in yoga-nidrā, he kicked the Lord's chest. Viṣṇu, instead of getting angry, apologized and shampooed Bhṛgu's foot. While Mahālakṣmī was offended (leading to the Venkateswara avatar), the mark remained as a sign of the Lord's supreme patience.

Śrī Kṛṣṇa, Narakāsura & the Pārijāta

Kṛṣṇa sits on a siṃhāsana (lion-throne, first used by Narasiṃha) under the Pārijāta Tree. This tree was brought from Indraloka after the battle with Narakāsura.

Battle Mystery: Narakāsura could only be killed by his mother. Satyabhāmā was an avatar of Bhūmidevī (his mother). Kṛṣṇa pretended to swoon so Satyabhāmā would take control and kill the demon.

The Mother's Mediation

Devotees approach Bhagavān through Mahālakṣmī. Like a child with a poor report card approaching the mother to recommend them to a stern father, Lakṣmī pleads for us, reminding the Lord that we are His children and have repented.

Spiritual Stages & Practical Remedies

Spiritual growth follows three steps: Śravaṇam (listening), Mananam (reflecting), and Nididhyāsanam (action/contemplation).

Practical Remedies

  • Financial Problems/Debt: Chant Śloka 65 (Lakṣmī names) 10 times daily, or the Rṇavimocana Ślokam.
  • Sama Veda Meters: Three primary sāmas are Bṛhat, Rathantara, and Vāmadevya.
  • Sitting Icon: Found in the Ramaswami Temple in Kumbakonam (Paṭṭābhiṣeka Rāma).

Kaṭākṣam: The Lord's glance that "burns off" (akṣam) bad karmas (kaṭa).

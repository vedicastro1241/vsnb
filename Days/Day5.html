The Technical Framework

Compiled by various ṛṣis across yugas, these names form a garland for Kṛṣṇa Paramātmā.

  • Ṛṣi: Vyāsa Bhagavān.
  • Chandas: Anuṣṭup (4 lines of 8 syllables).
  • Devatā: Śrī Kṛṣṇa.
The Purpose of Creation: Pralaya (annihilation) is a period of rest for the jīvātmā, much like sleep follows a day of work.

Analysis: "Śāntākāraṃ"

This dhyāna śloka provides a specific visualization to engage the mind:

  • Śāntākāraṃ: Perfectly peaceful form.
  • Bhujagaśayanaṃ: Resting on the serpent Ādiśeṣa.
  • Meghavarṇaṃ: Dark like rain clouds, showering compassion.
Lotus Eyes: His eyes reflect the form of Mahālakṣmī, who is eternally gazing at Him.

Initiation and the power of dedicated practice through historical examples.

  • Dhruva: Attained a vision of the Lord in six months via the 12-syllable mantra.
  • The Śrīvatsa Mark: The residence of Mahālakṣmī on the Lord's chest, marking His patience with Sage Bhṛgu.

The Grace of Śrī Kṛṣṇa

Kṛṣṇa sits on a golden siṃhāsana under the celestial pārijāta tree.

The Report Card Analogy: We approach Bhagavān through Mahālakṣmī. She pleads for the devotee, ensuring the Lord accepts us.

Remedies for Difficulties

  • Financial Debt: Chant Śloka 65 or the Rṇavimocana Ślokam.
  • Fear of Future: Meditate on Bhavabhayaharam.

Day 1: The Vedic Path: Ancient Theory

The Three Guṇas & Vedic Structure

The discourse explains the three qualities (guṇas) inherent in everyone:

  • Sattvaguṇa: Peace and happiness.
  • Rajoguṇa: Restlessness and action.
  • Tamoguṇa: Lethargy and laziness.
The Vedas are divided into Karmakāṇḍa (Performance) and Jñānakāṇḍa (Knowledge).

The Necessity of a Guru

The Vedas are indirect and "cryptic." A Guru is required to unlock the meaning of mantras like the Śānti Mantra.

Without guidance, profound statements about the "completeness" of the universe can seem illogical, but the Guru explains how the Divine remains whole even after creation.

Itihāsas: The Practical Examples

If the Vedas are the "Theory," the Itihāsas (Rāmāyaṇa and Mahābhārata) are the "Practical Laboratory."

While the Vedas say "Treat your father as God," Rāmacandra showed us how to do it by going to the forest for 14 years.

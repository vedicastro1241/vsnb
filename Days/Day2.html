Day 2: The Vedic Path to the Sahasranāmam

The Three Guṇas & Structure of the Vedas

The discourse begins by explaining the three types of guṇas (qualities) residing in every individual:

  • Sattvaguṇa: A calm, peaceful, and happy mind.
  • Rajoguṇa: Restlessness and an unending desire for action.
  • Tamoguṇa: Lethargy, laziness, and a lack of willingness to perform.

The Vedas provide means for fulfilling desires arising from these qualities. They are classified into saṃhitā, brāhmaṇas, āraṇyakas, and upaniṣads, divided into Karmakāṇḍa (action) and Jñānakāṇḍa (contemplation).

The Role of the Guru: The Vedas are indirect. A Guru is essential to interpret mantras like the Śānti Mantra, which describes how Bhagavān resides both in Vaikuṇṭha and throughout the 14 lokas.

The Mahābhārata & The Birth of the Sahasranāmam

The Mahābhārata is the Pañcamo Veda (Fifth Veda). It contains 125,000 ślokas. We have two "executive summaries": the Bhagavad Gītā and the Śrī Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam.

Origin: Found in the 149th chapter of the Anuśāsana Parva. After the war, Yudhiṣṭhira was led by Kṛṣṇa to Bhīṣma, who was lying on the bed of arrows (śarapañjara). Bhīṣma delivered the names in the direct presence of Śrī Kṛṣṇa.

Six Reasons for Greatness & The Lord's Form

Ādi Śaṅkarācārya cites six reasons for the text's supremacy:

  1. Mahābhārata-sāratvāt: Essence of the Fifth Veda.
  2. Ṛṣibhiḥ parigītatvāt: Chanted by ancient sages.
  3. Vedavyāsena grathitatvāt: Compiled by Vyāsa (Viṣṇu incarnation).
  4. Bhīṣma-parigrahāt: Final "ultimate opinion" of a supreme devotee.
  5. Sarva-parigrahāt: Accepted by all schools of thought.
  6. Gītā-samvādāt: Harmony with Kṛṣṇa's words.

Viṣvaksena, Vyāsa, and the Lineage

The Lineage: Nārāyaṇa → Brahmā → Vasiṣṭha → Śakti → Parāśara → Vyāsa → Śuka.

Conquering the Ṣaḍripu

Bhagavān is Jiṣṇu (The Victorious), having conquered the six internal enemies: Kāma, Krodha, Lobha, Moha, Mada, and Mātsarya.

Karma, Free Will, and Purity

Karma Types: Sañcita (accumulation) and Prārabdha (exhaustion).

Day 2: Pūrvāṅgam: Vighna-Nivāraṇa

The Three Guṇas & Vedic Structure

Human nature is governed by three guṇas (qualities). The proportion of these varies in every individual, and the Vedas provide the means to fulfill desires arising from them:

  • Sattvaguṇa: A calm, peaceful, and happy mind.
  • Rajoguṇa: Restlessness and an unending desire for action.
  • Tamoguṇa: Lethargy, laziness, and a lack of spiritual willingness.

The Vedas are classified into Saṃhitā, Brāhmaṇas, Āraṇyakas, and Upaniṣads. They are broadly divided into the Karmakāṇḍa (action) and the Jñānakāṇḍa (knowledge/contemplation).

The Role of the Guru

The Vedas are indirect. A Guru is essential to interpret mantras like the Śānti Mantra. Without depth, a literal translation of "completeness" might seem like blabbering; in reality, it describes how Bhagavān resides in both Vaikuṇṭha and all 14 lokas simultaneously.

The Itihāsas: Practical Proof

The ṛṣis recognized that theory is difficult for common people. They composed Purāṇas and Itihāsas as practical demonstrations of Vedic principles.

  • Rāmacandra: Practically demonstrated the Vedic command to worship one's father by entering the forest for 14 years.
  • Rāmāyaṇa & Gāyatrī: The Rāmāyaṇa’s 24,000 ślokas are structured so each 1,000 starts with a letter of the 24-syllable Gāyatrī mantra.

The "Executive Summary"

The Mahābhārata (Pañcamo Veda) contains 125,000 ślokas. For those in Kaliyuga who cannot read it all, there are two "executive summaries": the Bhagavad Gītā and the Śrī Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam.

The Sahasranāmam originates in the 149th chapter of the Anuśāsana Parva. It was given by Bhīṣma to Yudhiṣṭhira on the bed of arrows (śarapañjara) in the presence of Śrī Kṛṣṇa.

Six Reasons for Greatness

Ādi Śaṅkarācārya cites six specific reasons why this text is supreme:

  1. Mahābhārata-sāratvāt: Essence of the Fifth Veda.
  2. Ṛṣibhiḥ parigītatvāt: Chanted by ancient sages before compilation.
  3. Vedavyāsena grathitatvāt: Compiled by Vyāsa, an incarnation of Viṣṇu.
  4. Bhīṣma-parigrahāt: The ultimate opinion of a supreme devotee.
  5. Sarva-parigrahāt: Accepted by all scholars (even in medicine/Charaka Saṃhitā).
  6. Gītā-samvādāt: Harmony with the Gītā's Japa-yajña.

The Easiest Path in Kaliyuga

Unlike rituals requiring wealth or specific timing, Japam (chanting) can be done by anyone, anywhere. It is the most effective "shoes" to insulate yourself from the "thorns" of a wicked world.

The Six Preliminary Ślokas

Before the 1,000 names, we chant six ślokas covering: Vighna-nivāraṇa (removing obstacles), gratitude to Vyāsa, and meditation on Mahāviṣṇu.

Analysis of the First Śloka

śuklāmbaradharaṃ viṣṇuṃ śaśivarṇaṃ caturbhujam | prasannavadanaṃ dhyāyet sarvavighnopaśāntaye ||

We meditate on Him for the pacification of three types of obstacles (Vighnas):

  • Ādhyātmika: Internal obstacles like laziness.
  • Ādhidaivika: Natural obstacles (weather, technical failures).
  • Ādhibhautika: Obstacles caused by others (distractions).

Viṣvaksena & Vyāsa

Viṣvaksena: The CEO

Bhagavān delegated universe management to Viṣvaksena. He commands an army (dviradavaktrādyāḥ) that shatters obstacles. We worship him as a bhāgavata (supreme devotee).

Vyāsa Bhagavān: The Compiler

His name is Kṛṣṇa Dvaipāyana (born on an island). He is called Vyāsa because he provided the exposition (vyāsam) of the Vedas.

The Lineage: Nārāyaṇa → Brahmā → Vasiṣṭha → Śakti → Parāśara → Vyāsa → Śuka.

"Vyāsāya Viṣṇu rūpāya" — Vyāsa is a manifestation of Mahāviṣṇu, appearing in Dvāpara Yuga to classify knowledge for our benefit.

Karma and the Path to Bliss

Beings are trapped in the Saṃsāra-cakra (cycle of birth/death) due to karma:

  • Sañcita Karma: Accumulation of all past actions.
  • Prārabdha Karma: The subset selected for this current life.

Free Will: Like a cow tied to a rope, we have freedom within a radius. While events are predetermined, our reaction to them is our choice.

Purity in Daily Life

Dietary Control: Food affects the mind. The cook’s thoughts enter the food. Offer food as naivedya to sanctify it into prasāda.

Lunar Eclipse: Chanting during an eclipse is thousands of times more potent. Use the eclipse period for continuous Sahasranāmam chanting.

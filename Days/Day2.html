The Supremacy of Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam

The Supremacy of Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam and the Chanting Path

The Three Guṇas and the Structure of the Vedas

The discourse explains the three types of guṇas (qualities) that reside in every individual:

  • Sattvaguṇa: Characterized by a calm, peaceful, and happy mind.
  • Rajoguṇa: Marked by restlessness and an unending desire to take action.
  • Tamoguṇa: Defined by lethargy, laziness, and a lack of willingness to perform tasks.

The Vedas are classified into saṃhitā, brāhmaṇas, āraṇyakas, and upaniṣads. They are divided into the karmakāṇḍa (performance of actions) and the jñānakāṇḍa (contemplation on the purpose of life).

The Role of the Guru and Itihāsas

A Guru is essential to interpret the mantras. The ṛṣis composed the Purāṇas and Itihāsas as practical demonstrations of Vedic principles. For example, Rāmacandra demonstrated worshipping one's father as a god, and the Rāmāyaṇa (24,000 ślokas) is an incarnation of the Vedas structured around the Gāyatrī mantra.

The Mahābhārata (Pañcamo Veda) contains two "executive summaries": the Bhagavad Gītā and the Śrī Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam.

The Greatness of Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam

The Sahasranāmam is considered a pārāyaṇa (chanting), which is often more accessible than the anuśāsana (instruction) of the Gītā. Simply chanting it is a sufficient sādhana.

Six Reasons for Greatness (by Ādi Śaṅkarācārya)

  1. Mahābhārata-sāratvāt: Essence of the Mahābhārata.
  2. Ṛṣibhiḥ parigītatvāt: Chanted by great sages.
  3. Vedavyāsena grathitatvāt: Compiled by Vyāsa (incarnation of Viṣṇu).
  4. Bhīṣma-parigrahāt: Delivered by Bhīṣma as his final opinion.
  5. Sarva-parigrahāt: Accepted by all philosophical schools.
  6. Gītā-samvādāt: In harmony with Kṛṣṇa's words.

Analysis of the First Śloka

śuklāmbaradharaṃ viṣṇuṃ śaśivarṇaṃ caturbhujam | prasannavadanaṃ dhyāyet sarvavighnopaśāntaye ||

One meditates on Viṣṇu ("all-pervading") to pacify three types of obstacles (Vighnas): Ādhyātmika (internal), Ādhidaivika (nature), and Ādhibhautika (other beings).

The Divine Attributes: Viṣṇu, Viṣvaksena, and Vyāsa

Attributes: śuklāmbaradharaṃ (clothed by the sky), śaśivarṇaṃ (moon-colored), caturbhujam (four-armed), and prasannavadanaṃ (smiling face).

Viṣvaksena: The CEO

Bhagavān delegated the management of the universe to Viṣvaksena. He commands an army of attendants (dviradavaktrādyāḥ). Praying to such supreme devotees is a powerful way to remove obstacles.

Vyāsa Bhagavān

Born as Kṛṣṇa Dvaipāyana, he is an incarnation of Viṣṇu who classified the Vedas and wrote the Brahma Sūtras. His lineage: Nārāyaṇa → Brahmā → Vasiṣṭha → Śakti → Parāśara → Vyāsa → Śuka.

The Internal Conquest and Eternal Bliss

Bhagavān is Jiṣṇu (victorious), having conquered the six internal enemies (Ṣaḍripu):

  • Kāma (Desire), Krodha (Anger), Lobha (Greed), Moha (Delusion), Mada (Pride), and Mātsarya (Jealousy).

Nature of the Ātmā

The body undergoes change (Vikāra), but the Ātmā is pure and blissful (Ānanda). Desires act like clouds covering the sun; when removed, the natural bliss of the soul is experienced.

To break the Saṃsāra-cakra (cycle of birth/death), one must practice constant remembrance of Bhagavān so that He is naturally remembered at the final moment (Antyakāla).

Karma, Dharma, and the Path to Liberation

  • Sañcita Karma: Accumulation of all past actions.
  • Prārabdha Karma: The subset of karma being exhausted in the current life.

Free Will: While events are predetermined by karma (like a cow tied to a rope), how an individual reacts to those events is within their control. Using intellect to surrender ensures one stays on the right path.

Symbolism of Arghya

The "Sun" represents the jīvātmā. Offering water and chanting the Gāyatrī helps remove the delusion (manda) of identifying with the body.

The Gīta Way: Purity in Diet and Devotion

Spiritual progress begins with control of food. Food affects the mind; thus, a Sāttvika diet is advised. Offering food as naivedya and chanting while cooking turns food into prasāda, removing blemishes.

Lunar Eclipse Practice

Chanting during an eclipse is significantly more potent. During the eclipse (3:00 PM to 6:47 PM), one should continuously chant the names of the Lord, followed by a ritual bath (snāna).

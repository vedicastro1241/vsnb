Day 2: Pūrvāṅgam: Vighna-Nivāraṇa

The Three Guṇas & Vedic Structure

Human nature is governed by three guṇas. Every action we take is influenced by these internal "colors":

[Visual: The Three Gunas - Sattva (White), Rajas (Red), Tamas (Black)]
  • Sattvaguṇa: Clarity, peace, and spiritual focus.
  • Rajoguṇa: Passion, restlessness, and constant "doing."
  • Tamoguṇa: Inertia, sleep, and resistance to change.

The Vedas provide a roadmap to transition from Tamas to Sattva through the Karmakāṇḍa (action) and Jñānakāṇḍa (knowledge).

The Role of the Guru

A Guru is essential to interpret the depth of mantras. Without guidance, the Śānti Mantra might seem literal; with a Guru, we understand how Bhagavān resides in both Vaikuṇṭha and our hearts simultaneously.

The Itihāsas: Practical Proof

The ṛṣis composed Purāṇas and Itihāsas as practical demonstrations of Vedic principles.

  • Rāmāyaṇa: A demonstration of Dharma in personal relationships (Father-Son). Each 1,000 ślokas start with a letter of the Gāyatrī.

The "Executive Summary"

The Mahābhārata (Pañcamo Veda) contains 125,000 ślokas. For those in Kaliyuga, the two "executive summaries" are the Bhagavad Gītā and the Śrī Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam.

The Sahasranāmam was given by Bhīṣma to Yudhiṣṭhira on the bed of arrows (śarapañjara) in the presence of Śrī Kṛṣṇa.

The Shoes Metaphor: We cannot cover the whole world in leather to avoid thorns, but we can wear leather shoes. Similarly, Japa is the "shoe" that protects us from the thorns of the world.

Viṣvaksena & Vyāsa

Viṣvaksena: The CEO

Bhagavān delegated universe management to Viṣvaksena. He commands an army that shatters obstacles. We worship him as the supreme devotee and administrator.

Vyāsa Bhagavān: The Compiler

"Vyāsāya Viṣṇu rūpāya" — Vyāsa is a manifestation of Mahāviṣṇu, appearing to classify knowledge for our benefit.

The Lineage: Nārāyaṇa → Brahmā → Vasiṣṭha → Śakti → Parāśara → Vyāsa → Śuka.

Karma and the Path to Bliss

Beings are trapped in the Saṃsāra-cakra due to two types of ledger entries:

  • Sañcita Karma: The total accumulation of all past actions.
  • Prārabdha Karma: The specific portion meant for this current life.
[Visual: The Cow and Rope Metaphor for Free Will]

Free Will: While our rope (Karma) has a specific length, we have the freedom to move anywhere within that radius. Our reaction to events is our freedom.

Purity in Daily Life

Food affects the mind. Offer food as naivedya to turn it into prasāda, neutralizing the thoughts of the cook.

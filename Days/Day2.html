Day 2: From Ancient Theory to Sahasranāmam

The Three Guṇas

Every individual is a mix of three qualities:

  • Sattvaguṇa: Peace, happiness, clarity.
  • Rajoguṇa: Restlessness, constant desire for action.
  • Tamoguṇa: Lethargy, laziness, procrastination.

The Vedas address these through Karmakāṇḍa (action) and Jñānakāṇḍa (knowledge). A Guru is essential to decode these "indirect" mantras correctly.

Purāṇas & Itihāsas

The ṛṣis created these as "practical demonstrations" of Vedic theorems.

The Executive Summaries: The Mahābhārata (Pañcamo Veda) is summarized in the Bhagavad Gītā and the Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam.

Origin

Found in the 149th chapter of the Anuśāsana Parva, given by Bhīṣma to Yudhiṣṭhira on the bed of arrows (śarapañjara) in Kṛṣṇa's presence.

Why Chanting is Supreme

Ādi Śaṅkarācārya provides six reasons for its greatness:

Mahābhārata-sāratvātThe essence of the great epic.
Ṛṣibhiḥ parigītatvātSung by ancient sages across ages.
Vedavyāsena grathitatvātCompiled by Vyāsa (Viṣṇu Himself).
Bhīṣma-parigrahātThe final "ultimate opinion" of Bhīṣma.
Sarva-parigrahātAccepted by all schools and Charaka Saṃhitā.
Gītā-samvādātMatches Kṛṣṇa's "I am the chanting of names."

Unlike the Gītā (anuśāsana), which requires complex yoga, the Sahasranāmam is pārāyaṇa (chanting)—the easiest path in Kaliyuga.

The First Śloka & Obstacles

śuklāmbaradharaṃ viṣṇuṃ śaśivarṇaṃ caturbhujam...

Three Types of Vighnas (Obstacles)

  • Ādhyātmika: Internal (laziness, lack of interest).
  • Ādhidaivika: Nature (rain, technical/Wi-Fi issues).
  • Ādhibhautika: Others (distractions from people/pets).

Form: Viṣṇu wears white (śukla) or is "clothed by the sky" (ambara), with the soothing color of the moon (śaśivarṇaṃ).

The Divine Hierarchy

Viṣvaksena: The CEO

Bhagavān's "Chief Commanding Officer." In temples like Tirumala, officials report to him first. He commands the divine army (dviradavaktrādyāḥ).

Vyāsa Bhagavān

Born as Kṛṣṇa Dvaipāyana on an island. He is the great-grandson of Vasiṣṭha.
Lineage: Nārāyaṇa → Brahmā → Vasiṣṭha → Śakti → Parāśara → Vyāsa → Śuka.

"Vyāsāya Viṣṇu rūpāya" — Vyāsa is an incarnation of Viṣṇu.

Karma & Daily Life

Sañcita Karma is our accumulation; Prārabdha is the subset for this life. We are like a cow tied to a rope—limited but free to choose our reaction.

The Six Internal Enemies (Ṣaḍripu)

Kāma (Desire), Krodha (Anger), Lobha (Greed), Moha (Delusion), Mada (Pride), Mātsarya (Jealousy). Chanting helps us overcome these.

Purity in Food

The subtle part of food affects the mind. Offer food as naivedya to remove the "mental fingerprints" of the cook and turn it into prasāda.

Eclipse Practice: Chanting during a lunar eclipse is significantly more potent.

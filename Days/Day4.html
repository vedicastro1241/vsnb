Genesis, Dissolution & Fear

Establishing Bhagavān as the source of all. All jīvātmās emerge from Him and return during pralaya (dissolution).

The Return: Dissolution is not destruction; it is a return to an original form. Like a mud pot returning to mud, we return to the Lord.

Technical Components of the Mantra

The Viṣṇu Sahasranāmam is a structured mantra with specific spiritual "gears":

ComponentSignificance
Ṛṣi (The Seer)Veda Vyāsa. The visualizer.
Chandas (Meter)Anuṣṭup Chandas. 32 syllables total.
Devatā (Deity)Kṛṣṇa Paramātmā.
Bījam (Seed)Amṛtāṃśūdbhavaḥ.
Śakti (Strength)Devakīnandanaḥ.
Hṛdayam (Heart)The Sāma Veda.

Nyāsam: Installing the Divine

Nyāsam involves depositing divine power into the physical body. By invoking these aspects, we acknowledge that the body is a Temple.

The Holy Right Ear

The right ear is considered a universal purifier containing the holy Gaṅgā, released the river through Jahnu Maharṣi's ear.

The Chariot of Self-Control

To protect the "Temple of the Body," we invoke the Lord's weapons. The Śārṅga (Bow) represents the control of the senses.

  • The Horses: The 5 Senses.
  • The Reins: The Mind.
  • The Charioteer: The Intellect (Buddhi).

When the Intellect (Kṛṣṇa) holds the mind in check, the practitioner reaches the destination safely.

Sincerity in Daily Motion

Is it okay to chant while working or driving? The story of Nārada and the Farmer provides the answer.

The Lesson: A farmer who remembers God briefly while tending his duties is as dear to the Lord as a sage who chants 24/7.

Chanting Tips:

  • Location: Any place—car, kitchen, or office.
  • State: Sincerity and bhakti are the primary requirements.
